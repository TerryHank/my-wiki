# Alex Krizhevsky

Alex Krizhevsky is a researcher in the field of computer vision and deep learning. He is best known for creating the **CIFAR-10** dataset and authoring the seminal 2009 technical report *"Learning multiple layers of features from tiny images"*.

His work on CIFAR-10 provided a standardized benchmark that has been instrumental in the development and evaluation of [[convolutional-neural-networks]] (CNNs) and other image classification algorithms. The dataset remains a standard tool for validating new model architectures due to its balance of simplicity and diversity.
